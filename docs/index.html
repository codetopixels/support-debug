Privacy Policy — Support Debug Report

Last updated: 7 July 2026

Support Debug Report is a browser extension that helps you share technical details with a support team. When you click the extension icon, it builds a plain-text report and copies it to your clipboard. That is all it does.

What the extension collects

Nothing is collected, stored, or transmitted by the extension or its developer. The extension has no server and makes no network requests of its own.

To build the report, the extension reads — on your device only:

  • Errors that occur on the web page you are viewing: console errors and warnings, uncaught JavaScript errors, failed network requests, and failed resource loads.
  • Basic details about the page: its address (URL), title, and window size.
  • Basic details about your browser and system: browser name and version, operating system, screen size, language, and timezone.

This information is held only in the memory of the page you are viewing and is discarded when you close or leave the page.

Where the report goes

The report is copied to your clipboard only when you click the extension icon. Nothing is sent anywhere by the extension. You decide who to share the report with, by pasting it yourself (for example into an email or chat with a support team).

Before pasting, you can review exactly what was copied using "View what was copied" in the extension popup.

Permissions

The extension asks to read data on the websites you visit. This is required because errors can only be recorded at the moment they happen — they cannot be read after the fact — so the extension must be running on a page before a problem occurs. It does not modify pages, track browsing history, or send any data off your device.
